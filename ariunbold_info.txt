── HARDWARE / CREATIVE ENGINEERING PROJECTS ─────────────────

ESP32 Microcontroller Integration:
   - Developed firmware in C++ for efficient performance.
   - Implemented U8g2 library for binary-encoded GIFs on a 128x64
     OLED display.
   - Optimized resources for smooth playback on limited hardware.
   Detail (ESP32 case-study page):
   - Dev environment built on Arch Linux; wrestled with drivers
     and toolchain configuration for ESP32 compilation.
   - Biggest challenge: fitting animation onto a 128x64 pixel OLED.
     Color dropped entirely; every frame converted to monochrome
     binary data; resolution reduced to fit ESP32's limited memory.
     Custom rendering pipeline (C++, U8g2) decodes binary-encoded
     GIF frames and pushes them to the display at smooth framerates.
     A demo posted on Instagram pulled solid views.
   - Wiring: ESP32 to SSD1306 OLED via I2C — 4 wires (VCC, GND,
     SDA, SCL).

Project: CryoCell (Samsung Galaxy S21 hardware mod):
   - Engineered a custom 10,000mAh external battery solution.
   - Designed a fan control module with Power Save / Performance
     modes.
   - Showcases hardware modification and thermal management skills.
   Detail (S21 mod case-study page):
   - Section 1 — Active Cooling Architecture: Standard passive
     cooling on the S21 wasn't enough for sustained peak
     performance. Mapped thermal hotspots on the chassis and
     carved a precise circular mount for a custom cooling fan
     assembly sitting flush against the device. Lowered device
     temperature by 10°C, maintaining stability under max load.
   - Section 2 — Power Delivery / Bypassing Limitations: Integrated
     a 10,000mAh external power bank directly into the S21's
     internal circuitry. Soldered the custom fan assembly to a
     dedicated controller, creating dual operational modes: Power
     Save and Performance. Direct wiring bypasses standard
     hardware limitations for extended, uninterrupted battery life.
